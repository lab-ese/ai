1. PSEUDO PY CODE:
import math
from collections import Counter

def calculate_similarity(text1, text2):
    tokens1 = text1.lower().split()
    tokens2 = text2.lower().split()
    
    freq1 = Counter(tokens1)
    freq2 = Counter(tokens2)
    
    # Cosine Similarity
    all_words = set(freq1.keys()).union(set(freq2.keys()))
    dot_product = sum(freq1.get(w, 0) * freq2.get(w, 0) for w in all_words)
    mag1 = math.sqrt(sum(v**2 for v in freq1.values()))
    mag2 = math.sqrt(sum(v**2 for v in freq2.values()))
    cosine = dot_product / (mag1 * mag2)
    
    # Jaccard Similarity
    set1, set2 = set(tokens1), set(tokens2)
    intersection = len(set1.intersection(set2))
    union = len(set1.union(set2))
    jaccard = intersection / union
    
    return cosine, jaccard

2. ALGORITHM IN STEPS:
Step 1: Convert both input texts to lowercase and split them into lists of words (tokens).
Step 2: Cosine Similarity: Calculate the frequency of each word in both texts.
Step 3: Calculate the dot product by multiplying frequencies of matching words and summing them up.
Step 4: Calculate the magnitude of both frequency vectors (square root of sum of squared frequencies).
Step 5: Divide dot product by the product of magnitudes.
Step 6: Jaccard Similarity: Convert both token lists into mathematical Sets (removes duplicates).
Step 7: Find the Intersection (count of common words) and Union (count of total unique words).
Step 8: Divide Intersection by Union.

3. DETAILED DRY RUN:
Text 1: "apple banana"
Text 2: "banana cherry"
Freq1: {"apple":1, "banana":1}. Freq2: {"banana":1, "cherry":1}
Cosine:
- Dot Product: (1*0) + (1*1) + (0*1) = 1
- Mag1: sqrt(1^2 + 1^2) = 1.414. Mag2: 1.414.
- Cosine = 1 / (1.414 * 1.414) = 0.5
Jaccard:
- Set1: {"apple", "banana"}, Set2: {"banana", "cherry"}
- Intersection: {"banana"} -> size 1
- Union: {"apple", "banana", "cherry"} -> size 3
- Jaccard = 1 / 3 = 0.333
